Why we built this

Color confidence without the consultation fee

Have you ever bought an outfit that looked amazing in the store, only to get home and realise it made your skin look dull or tired? Or spent money on a hair colour that just didn't quite work, even though it looked great on someone else? Most of us have been there.

The reason is usually simple — the color didn't match your undertone. Not your skin tone, but your undertone — the subtle warm, cool, or neutral hue that sits beneath the surface of your skin and never changes. It's the same principle professional stylists and color consultants have been using for decades, but getting a real consultation typically costs $100 to $300 and requires booking an appointment weeks in advance.

We wanted to change that. AI Color Analysis was created to give anyone — regardless of budget or location — access to the same insights that used to be reserved for people who could afford a personal stylist. Upload a photo, and in seconds you'll know your undertone, your seasonal color type, and which colors actually suit you.

How it works

Simple analysis, useful results

When you upload a photo, our tool analyses the pixel data from your facial skin regions — specifically areas like your cheeks and forehead where your true skin color shows most clearly. It measures the balance of warm and cool tones in those pixels, determines your undertone (warm, cool, or neutral), and then maps that to a seasonal color profile: Spring, Summer, Autumn, or Winter.

From there, it generates a personal color palette — clothing shades, hair color suggestions, and jewelry metals that are proven to complement your specific complexion. Everything runs directly in your browser. Your photo is never uploaded to any server or stored anywhere. It stays on your device the entire time.

Who it's for

Designed for confident everyday choices

AI Color Analysis is for anyone who wants to feel more confident about the colors they wear — whether you're building a new wardrobe, thinking about changing your hair, choosing makeup, or just curious about why certain outfits always seem to get compliments and others don't.

It's especially useful if you've never had a professional color consultation before and aren't sure where to start. The tool gives you a clear, simple result you can actually use — not a complicated theory, just practical guidance you can apply the next time you go shopping.

Privacy first

Our approach to privacy

We take privacy seriously, especially when it involves something as personal as a photo of your face. That's why we designed AI Color Analysis to work entirely on your device. When you upload a photo, it is processed locally in your browser using JavaScript — it is never sent to our servers, never stored in a database, and never seen by anyone else. The moment you close or refresh the page, the photo is gone.

We don't require you to create an account, verify an email, or hand over any personal information to use the tool. Just upload, analyse, and get your results. That's it.

Accuracy matters

A note on getting the best result

Color analysis works best in natural daylight with a clear, unfiltered photo. Heavy filters, indoor yellow lighting, thick makeup, or low-resolution images can affect how accurately the tool reads your undertone. For the most reliable result, we recommend using a recent selfie taken near a window in natural light, with minimal makeup and no color filters applied.

The tool is designed to be a helpful guide, not a medical or professional diagnosis. Color analysis is inherently subjective, and your personal taste and preferences always matter. Use the results as a starting point and experiment from there — fashion is meant to be fun.

Our mission

Making personal color analysis genuinely accessible

Personal color analysis has existed as a concept since the 1980s, but it has always been something most people only read about or heard stylists mention. We want to make it genuinely accessible — free, instant, private, and easy enough for anyone to use from their phone in under a minute.

If this tool helps even one person stop buying clothes that don't suit them, or finally find a hair color they actually love, then it's doing exactly what it was built to do.
